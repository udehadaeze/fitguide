# Predefined Evaluation Relevance

These rules were written before running the Phase 4 evaluation. They use only synthetic catalogue attributes and therefore measure internal prototype relevance rather than user benefit or clinical suitability.

P01 is relevant when an item is beginner-level, low impact, usable at home with available equipment, free from jumping, and marked for general fitness. P02 is relevant when an item is no harder than intermediate, usable at the gym with available equipment, targets upper body or core, and has a strength or core category with strength support. P03 is relevant when an item works outdoors, requires no unavailable equipment, is no harder than intermediate, and supports cardiovascular fitness through a cardio category.

P04 is relevant when an item is beginner-level and low impact, uses no floor or kneeling position, works at home, and belongs to mobility or flexibility while addressing at least one selected body area. P05 is relevant when an item has no overhead or single-leg balance requirement, is not Walking in Place, uses available home equipment, and belongs to a selected category. P06 is relevant when an item is low impact, belongs to relaxation, flexibility or mobility, and carries the stress-reduction flag.

P07 is relevant when an item has neither jumping nor high impact, works at the gym with available equipment, is no harder than advanced, and supports endurance through cardio or strength. P08 is expected to return no result because its location, duration, equipment and restriction combination intentionally removes every compatible catalogue item. It is evaluated for safe empty-result handling rather than Precision@5.
